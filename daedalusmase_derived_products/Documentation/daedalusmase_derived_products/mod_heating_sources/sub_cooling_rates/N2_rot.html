sub_Heating_Sources.N2_rot

Description:

Calculate loss due to N2 rotational excitation

$$\textbf{Schunk and Nagy}$$

$$L_e(N2,rot)= 3.5 \times 10^{-14} n_e n_{N2} (T_e - T_n) / T_e^{1/2} $$

$$\textbf{Rees and Roble}$$

$$L_e(N2,rot)= 2.9 \times 10^{-14} n_e n_{N2} (T_e - T_n) / T_e^{1/2} $$

Inputs:

Necm: electron density in (cm^{-3})

NN2: (N_2) density in (cm^{-3})

Te: electron temperature in K

Tn: neutral temperature in K

Outputs:

Le_N2_rotf_schunk: Accorsing to Schunk and Nagy [2009]

Le_N2_rotf_rees: According to Rees [1989]

Le_N2_rotf_tiegcm: According to TIEGCM formulas

Reference:

Schunk, R. and Nagy, A. (2009). Ionospheres: physics, plasma physics, and chemistry (Cambridge university press)

Rees, M. H. (1989). Physics and chemistry of the upper atmosphere. Cambridge University Press.

**Description**:

import numpy as np

def N2_rot(Necm,NN2,Te,Tn):
    
    #Schunk and Nagy
    Le_N2_rotf_schunk=(3.5*10**(-14)*Necm*NN2*(Te-Tn)/np.sqrt(Te))*1.60217662*10**(-13)  #W/m^3
    #Rees and Roble 
    Le_N2_rotf_rees=(2.9*10**(-14)*Necm*NN2*(Te-Tn)/np.sqrt(Te))*1.60217662*10**(-13)
    #TIEGCM
    Le_N2_rotf_tiegcm=(2.9*10**(-14)*Necm*NN2*(Te-Tn)/np.sqrt(Te))*1.60217662*10**(-13)
    
    
    return Le_N2_rotf_schunk,Le_N2_rotf_rees,Le_N2_rotf_tiegcm
